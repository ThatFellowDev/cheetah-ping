Check frequency determines how often Cheetah Ping fetches your monitored page and checks for changes.

## Available frequencies

| Frequency | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Every minute | Fastest. Ideal for time-sensitive changes. |
| Every 5 minutes | Good for price drops and stock monitoring. |
| Every 15 minutes | Balanced speed and efficiency. |
| Every 30 minutes | Good for moderately time-sensitive pages. |
| Every hour | Standard for most use cases. |
| Every 6 hours | Good for pages that change infrequently. |
| Every 24 hours | Daily check. Good for content pages. |
| Every week | Weekly check for slow-changing pages. |

## Frequency by plan

Your plan determines the fastest check interval available to you.

| Plan | Minimum interval | Monitors |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| Free ($0/mo) | Every 24 hours | Up to 5 |
| Starter ($9/mo) | Every 15 minutes | Up to 10 |
| Pro ($19/mo) | Every 5 minutes | Up to 50 |
| Ultra ($49/mo) | Every minute | Up to 50 |

## Choosing the right frequency

- **Price monitoring**: Every 5 to 15 minutes catches most price changes before they sell out.
- **Job postings**: Every hour is usually fast enough. New postings rarely disappear within minutes.
- **Legal/policy pages**: Daily or weekly. These pages change infrequently.
- **Competitor tracking**: Every 6 hours gives you same-day visibility without excessive checks.

The AI suggests a frequency when you create a monitor based on the page type and content. You can always adjust it later from the monitor detail page.
